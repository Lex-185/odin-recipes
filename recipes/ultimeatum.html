The Ulti-meatum

A burger so rare that it's served only once in a century

Prepared by head chef Ajay Maldonaldo from the Grill Em' Up Truck, the Ulti-Meatum is a burger cooked inside another burger. A symbol of health.

Only one is given out per customer and anyone who doesn't eat one... is a chump.

Ingredients:

The burger:

  • 2 types of cheese
  • Beef
  • Pastrami
  • Hamburger buns

Himalayan sauce:

  • Olive oil(1 tbsp)
  • Ginger
  • Tumeric
  • Chili
  • Garlic
  • Cinnamon(1/4 tsp)
  • Chili powder(2 tsp)
  • Mustard seed(1/2 tsp)
  • Cumin(1/2)
  • Coriander(1/2)
  • Nigella seed(1/2)
  • Malt Vinegar(1/2 cup)
  • Crushed tomatoes
  • Brown Sugar(1/2 tsp)
  • Himalayan salt(1 tsp)

Instructions:

Special ketchup:

  1. Prepare ginger, tumeric, chili, chili powder, garlic, cinnamon, mustard seed, cumin, coriander, nigella seed, vinegar and crushed tomatoes on a board.
  2. Saute ingredients in olive oil for a few minutes.
  3. Add a bit of malt vinegar and a can of crushed tomatoes.
  4. Let the mixture simmer for at least an hour.
  5. Pour the mixture into a blender and season with himalayan salt.

Ulti-meatum:

  1. Pan-fry burgers seasoned with salt and pepper.
  2. Toast the buns, add the cheese and create the first burger.
  3. Wrap the burger inside of another patty.
  4. Bake until it's cooked through.
  5. Deep fry the other burgers.
  6. Add the source to the buns.
  7. Fry the pastrami and bacon.
  8. Lay the pastrami and bacon on top of the wrapped burger.
  9. Last call for burgers. I repeat, last call for burgers!
